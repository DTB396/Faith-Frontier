# Contingency Representation Guidance

## For Attorneys and Pro Se Litigants

**Contingency Fee Intake Best Practices:**

- Review all filings and evidence above for case merits and damages.
- Assess the likelihood of recovery and the existence of collectible assets or insurance.
- Confirm the clientâ€™s indigency status and need for contingency representation.
- Use a written contingency fee agreement compliant with New Jersey Court Rules (see NJ Court Rule 1:21-7).
- Clearly define the scope of representation, fee percentage, and costs responsibility.
- Advise the client of all risks, alternatives, and the right to independent counsel.
- File a Notice of Appearance and promptly communicate with the court and opposing counsel.

**Persuasion Tips for Intake:**

- Emphasize the public interest, legal clarity, and potential for precedent.
- Highlight any procedural errors, due process violations, or egregious conduct by opposing parties.
- Provide a concise, lawyer-friendly summary (see above) and organize filings for rapid review.
- Be transparent about case status, deadlines, and any urgent relief sought.
